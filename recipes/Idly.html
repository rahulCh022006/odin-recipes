Idly (Steamed Rice Cake)

Description:

Idly is a traditional South Indian dish made from fermented rice and urad dal batter. These soft, fluffy rice cakes are typically steamed and served with chutneys and sambar. Idly is a healthy and light breakfast option, popular in Indian homes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)

Steps:

  1. Rinse the rice and urad dal separately and soak them with fenugreek seeds for about 6-8 hours.
  2. After soaking, grind the rice and dal into a smooth batter by adding enough water.
  3. Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours.
  4. Grease the idly molds with a little oil and pour the batter into them.
  5. Steam the idlis for about 10-12 minutes or until they are soft and fluffy.
  6. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
