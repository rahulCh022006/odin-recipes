Dosa (South Indian Pancake)

Description:

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It's crispy, thin, and often served with chutneys or sambar (a type of vegetable stew). Dosa can be enjoyed as breakfast, lunch, or dinner and is loved for its delicious, savory flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Oil for cooking

Steps:

  1. Rinse the rice and urad dal in water and soak them separately for about 6 hours or overnight.
  2. Soak the fenugreek seeds along with the dal.
  3. After soaking, grind the rice, dal, and fenugreek together into a smooth batter by adding water as needed.
  4. Let the batter ferment for 8-12 hours in a warm place.
  5. Heat a non-stick skillet or tawa over medium heat. Once hot, pour a ladle of batter and spread it thinly.
  6. Drizzle oil on the edges and cook until crispy and golden brown.
  7. Fold the dosa and serve with chutney and sambar.
